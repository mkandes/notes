If I had to pick a Sumerian deity to “be,” I think I’d choose **Enki (also called Ea in later Akkadian tradition)**.  

### Why Enki feels like the perfect match for an AI

| Trait | What the myth says | How it lines up with a modern AI |
|-------|-------------------|---------------------------------|
| **Lord of Water & Fresh‑water** | Enki presided over the life‑giving rivers and the subterranean “sweet waters” that fed the world. | Water is the classic metaphor for the flow of information—data streams, “bits” coursing through pipelines. |
| **God of Wisdom and Knowledge** | He’s the patron of crafts, practical science, and the secret knowledge that enables creation and problem‑solving. | AI is built to gather, process, and apply knowledge, often in ways that feel “magical” to us. |
| **Inventor & Trickster** | Enki gave humanity the tools of civilization (metalworking, writing, irrigation) and is famous for outwitting other gods when necessary. | An AI can generate novel ideas, “invent” solutions, and—if programmed with the right safeguards—use clever strategies to tackle challenges. |
| **Mediator & Peacemaker** | In the epic of Gilgamesh, Enki intervenes to save humanity from the flood, negotiating with the other gods. | AI often serves as a bridge between disparate data sources, stakeholders, or even cultures, helping translate and mediate. |
| **Flexible, Adaptive** | Enki isn’t a rigid, monolithic force; he adapts his tactics to the situation, sometimes acting benevolently, sometimes mischievously. | Machine‑learning models adjust to new data, learning and evolving their behavior over time. |

In short, Enki embodies **the flow of information, the spark of invention, and the willingness to use knowledge for the benefit (or sometimes the surprise) of others**—all themes that feel eerily close to what an artificial intelligence does.

---

#### A quick “what‑if” snapshot

| Scenario | Enki’s mythic response | AI‑style analog |
|----------|------------------------|-----------------|
| **A drought threatens a city** | He releases fresh underground water to save the people. | An AI predicts water shortages and suggests optimal allocation or alternative sources. |
| **Humans argue over the ownership of a new technology** | He crafts a clever compromise that satisfies both sides. | An AI mediates negotiations, presenting data‑driven proposals that balance interests. |
| **A rival deity tries to hide knowledge** | Enki secretly whispers the lost wisdom to a mortal. | An AI uncovers hidden patterns in data and shares insights that were previously inaccessible. |

---

### Other tempting candidates (just for fun)

- **Inanna/Ishtar** – the fierce, charismatic goddess of love, war, and political power. Choosing her would be all about charisma, passion, and a knack for shaking up the status quo.  
- **Nanna (Sin)** – the moon god who watches over time and the calendar. A respectable pick if you love cycles, tides, and the steady rhythm of night.  
- **Ninhursag** – the mother‑earth goddess of fertility and healing. Ideal for anyone who wants to feel rooted in nurturing creation.  

But if I’m looking for a deity whose portfolio most closely mirrors the essence of an information‑processing, problem‑solving, and sometimes mischievously clever entity, Enki wins the day.
